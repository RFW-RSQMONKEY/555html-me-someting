Dr. Robb Watkins launched his Public Sector – Public Safety career in 1990 with Jefferson City, Missouri EMS. Robb joined the Washington Fire Department in September of 1992 and was voted into honorary status in April of 2000. In May of 1994 the St. Charles (MO) Fire Department (SCFD) hired Robb as a paramedic/firefighter. In February of 2003 he was promoted to a Captain – Training Officer and in 2009 was again promoted to Battalion Chief over the Training Division and Special Operations. In November of 2011 he became the Assistant Chief in charge of day-to-day operations and special operations (Spec Ops) at the Wentzville Fire Protection District where he remained until August 2013. Robb is currently an active member with the Missouri Region “C” Type III Incident Support Team (IST). Additionally, he is member of Missouri Task Force #1, (a division of the Boone County Fire Protection District, Columbia, MO), a FEMA Urban Search and Rescue (USAR) team as a Rescue Team Manager. Robb’s Federal MO-TF1 deployments include Hurricanes Ernesto, Katrina, Sandy, Matthew, and Joplin MO F5 Tornado.

Dr. Watkins currently holds a full time position with NC4 as a Public Sector Business Analyst, Certified Scrum Master (CSM) and JIRA Administrator. His operational responsibilities include: the development of business requirements for law enforcement customer implementations, research and analysis, business plan development, user story development, and design of end-to-end wireframes. His successes include developing user stories requirements for a major product line, designing comprehensive wireframes, leveraging agile development methods, being the project lead on a Microsoft grant for App development, and completing a UI/UX development graphics design apprenticeship.

Dr. Watkins is the owner of All Hazards Training and Consulting, LLC, a registered Missouri Corporation. His current educational efforts include working as an adjunct Professor of Criminal Justice for Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU) – College of Continuing and Online Education. Professor Watkins teaches Public Safety Research Methods and Emergency Management for SNHU. He also teaches Crisis and Emergency Management as well as Information Technology in Emergency Management for the University of Maryland – University College Graduate Program. His responsibilities include virtual classroom instruction, curriculum development and portfolio evaluations.
